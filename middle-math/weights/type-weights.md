## 🛒 Push

**Character:** Chest, front deltoids, triceps. Horizontal pressing (bench, cable fly, dip) and vertical pressing (overhead press, landmine press, pike push-up). Exercise library: Section C (Chest), Section B (Shoulders — anterior/lateral), Section E (Arms — triceps, #51–95).

Self: +8 when primary Type

### Affinities

| Emoji | Weight | Source Rule |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| 🏛 Basics | +4 | Barbell bench press and barbell overhead press are the defining bilateral barbell classics for Push. scl-directory.md: 🏛 = "Barbell > dumbbell. Bilateral > unilateral. Compound > isolation." |
| ⛽ Strength | +4 | Bench press and overhead press are primary strength benchmarks. Horizontal and vertical pressing under heavy load (75–85%) are canonical Push expressions. scl-directory.md: ⛽ = "Heavy loads, low reps, force production." |
| 🦋 Hypertrophy | +4 | Chest and shoulder volume training (cable flyes, dumbbell press, machine press) is a core 🦋 Push application. The pump in pressing is well-documented. scl-directory.md: 🦋 = "Muscle growth. Volume and metabolic stress. The pump matters." |
| 🖼 Restoration | +3 | "🖼🛒 = shoulder mobility, thoracic extension." Push Type in Restoration focuses on anterior chain release and pressing-pattern mobility. scl-directory.md: 🖼 × Type note. |
| ⚖ Balance | +3 | "⚖🛒 = triceps, side delts, front delts." Balance zooms in on Push's weaker secondary muscles. scl-directory.md: ⚖ × Type note. |
| 🧈 Bread & Butter | +8 | "Always present. Most volume. Most stimulus." Universal. scl-directory.md: 🧈 definition. |
| 🤌 facio | +3 | "Execute, perform, produce. The concentric. The doing." The concentric push — pressing the weight — is the defining motion of Push Type. scl-directory.md: 🤌 definition. |
| 🚀 mitto | +3 | "Dispatch, deploy, launch, commit. Explosive intent." Plyometric push variations (plyo push-up, explosive overhead press) and max-effort presses are Push expressions. scl-directory.md: 🚀 definition. |

### Suppressions

| Emoji | Weight | Source Rule |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| 🪡 Pull | -6 | Push and Pull are direct antagonists: pressing vs. pulling, anterior chain vs. posterior chain. Selecting Pull exercises in a Push workout violates Type accuracy. scl-directory.md: 🛒 = "Chest, front delts, triceps" vs. 🪡 = "Lats, rear delts, biceps, traps, erectors." |
| 🍗 Legs | -5 | Lower body tissue is entirely outside Push's muscle group scope. scl-directory.md: 🛒 muscle group definition vs. 🍗 = "Quads, hamstrings, glutes, calves." |
| ➖ Ultra | -4 | Cardiovascular conditioning is not a pressing-pattern Type. scl-directory.md: ➖ = "Cardiovascular system, energy systems, endurance." Push muscles are not the primary tissue in rowing, running, or cycling. |

## 🪡 Pull

**Character:** Lats, rear deltoids, biceps, traps, erector spinae. Row patterns, pulldown patterns, hinge patterns (deadlift, RDL). Exercise library: Section D (Back), Section B (Shoulders — posterior/rotator cuff), Section E (Arms — biceps, #1–50), Section G (Hips — hip hinge patterns).

Self: +8 when primary Type

### Affinities

| Emoji | Weight | Source Rule |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| 🏛 Basics | +4 | Barbell deadlift, barbell row, and weighted pull-up are the bilateral barbell classics for Pull. scl-directory.md: 🏛 = "Barbell > dumbbell. Bilateral > unilateral. Compound > isolation. Classic > novel." |
| ⛽ Strength | +4 | Deadlift and barbell row are primary strength benchmarks. Heavy pulling under 75–85% is canonical Pull strength work. scl-directory.md: ⛽ = "Neural adaptation. Heavy loads, low reps, force production." |
| 🦋 Hypertrophy | +4 | Back volume (cable rows, lat pulldowns, dumbbell rows, machine pullovers) and bicep isolation are core 🦋 Pull applications. scl-directory.md: 🦋 = "Muscle growth. Volume. The pump matters." |
| 🖼 Restoration | +3 | "🖼🪡 = posterior chain tension release." Pull Type in Restoration: lat stretch, posterior capsule mobility, thoracic rotation, erector decompression. scl-directory.md: 🖼 × Type note. |
| ⚖ Balance | +3 | "⚖🪡 = biceps, rear delts, forearms, grip." Balance zooms in on Pull's secondary muscles — the ones often undertrained in push-dominant programs. scl-directory.md: ⚖ × Type note. |
| 🧈 Bread & Butter | +8 | "Always present. Most volume. Most stimulus." Universal. scl-directory.md: 🧈 definition. |
| 🧲 capio | +3 | "Receive, assess, intake. The catching phase. Absorbing the eccentric." The eccentric phase of pulling movements — controlled lowering on a row or pulldown — is the signal for growth. scl-directory.md: 🧲 definition. |
| 🥨 tendo | +3 | "Stretch, lengthen, push limits. Extend ROM." Full ROM pulling — arms long at the bottom of a row, full stretch in the lat on a pulldown — is a defining cue. scl-directory.md: 🥨 definition. |

### Suppressions

| Emoji | Weight | Source Rule |

| 🍗 Legs | -5 | Lower body tissue is outside Pull's scope (with the exception of hip hinge patterns in Section G, which Pull legitimately shares). scl-directory.md: 🪡 muscle group definition. |

Exercise library: Section F (Core), Section J (Olympic Lifts — GOLD required), Section K (Plyometrics — GOLD required), Section L (Kettlebell), Section Q (Strongman — GOLD required).

Self: +8 when primary Type

### Affinities

| Emoji | Weight | Source Rule |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| 🏟 Performance | +6 | "Full body power production." Olympic lifting tests and power benchmarks (snatch, clean and jerk, max broad jump) are Performance's native Plus expressions. scl-directory.md: 🏟 = "Strength benchmarks, conditioning benchmarks, movement assessments." |
| 🔴 Intense | +5 | "Maximum effort. High volume." GOLD unlocked. Plyometric and Olympic work under 🔴 is Plus's most expressive format. scl-directory.md: 🔴 = "GOLD: Yes." Plus Type requires 🔴 or 🟣 for GOLD sections. |
| 🟣 Technical | +5 | "Precision. Lower volume, extended rest, quality focus. GOLD: Yes." Olympic lift technique is the most technical expression of Plus. Fewer reps, longer rest, maximal precision. scl-directory.md: 🟣 = "GOLD: Yes. Session ends when quality degrades." |
| 🖼 Restoration | +3 | "🖼➕ = core as breath system, diaphragm, TVA." Plus in Restoration is diaphragmatic breathing, TVA activation, anti-rotation holds. scl-directory.md: 🖼 × Type note. |
| ⚖ Balance | +3 | "⚖➕ = rotational stability, anti-rotation." Balance zooms in on Plus's stabilization demands. scl-directory.md: ⚖ × Type note. |
| 🧈 Bread & Butter | +8 | "Always present. Most volume. Most stimulus." Universal. scl-directory.md: 🧈 definition. |

## ➖ Ultra

**Character:** Cardiovascular system, energy systems, endurance. Rowing, cycling, running, swimming, sled work, conditioning circuits, mobility flows. Exercise library: Section M (Cardio & Conditioning), Section O (Footwork & Agility), Section N (Sport Focused), Section K (Plyometrics — conditioning use).

Self: +8 when primary Type

### Affinities

| Emoji | Weight | Source Rule |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| ⌛ Time | +6 | "Enables EMOM, AMRAP, density blocks, timed sets, time trials, TUT, steady state, zone work." All conditioning work is measured in time. Time is Ultra's native axis. scl-directory.md: ⌛ axis definition. |
| 🖼 Restoration | +4 | "🖼➖ = nervous system regulation, somatic work." Active recovery (zone 2 aerobic work, gentle mobility flows) is a valid Restoration expression of Ultra. scl-directory.md: 🖼 × Type note. |
| ⚖ Balance | +4 | "⚖➖ = energy system gaps, aerobic base." Aerobic base building is a form of correction — filling in the cardiovascular deficit. scl-directory.md: ⚖ × Type note. |
| 🌾 Full Body | +4 | Conditioning work that integrates movement patterns (rowing, assault bike, sled push) complements full-body integration. ➖ can serve as the conditioning component of Full Body sessions. scl-directory.md: 🌾 = "Integration. Movements flowing together." |
| 🟠 Circuit | +4 | "Station-based timed rotation." Conditioning circuits (assault bike / rower / ski erg rotation) are Ultra's native circuit expression. scl-directory.md: 🟠 = "Station-based, timed rotation." |
| 🔴 Intense | +5 | "Maximum effort. High volume. Reduced rest." High-intensity interval work (HIIT, AMRAPs, Tabata intervals) is Ultra's most expressive format. scl-directory.md: 🔴 = "Maximum effort. High volume. Reduced rest 30–60s." |
| 🐋 duco | +4 | "Orchestrate, lead, conduct. Session architecture and tempo flow." Pacing and energy system management are the defining cognitive skills of cardiovascular training. scl-directory.md: 🐋 definition. |
| ✒️ grapho | +4 | "Write, program, prescribe, document. Record the set. Log the PR." Splits, watts, pace per 500m — all conditioning metrics that demand documentation. scl-directory.md: ✒️ definition. |
| 🧈 Bread & Butter | +8 | "Always present. Most volume. Most stimulus." Universal. scl-directory.md: 🧈 definition. |

### Suppressions

| Emoji | Weight | Source Rule |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| ⛽ Strength | -4 | "Neural adaptation. Heavy loads, low reps, full recovery." Ultra's training effect (cardiovascular and aerobic adaptation) is the opposite of strength's neural force-production adaptation. scl-directory.md: ⛽ = "Force production, not bodybuilding. The pump is irrelevant." |
| 🏛 Basics | -3 | "Barbell classics first." Bilateral barbell work is not the native tool of cardiovascular conditioning. scl-directory.md: 🏛 = "Barbell > dumbbell. Bilateral > unilateral. Compound > isolation." |
| 🪞 Vanity | -4 | "Appearance-driven. Pump work. Mirror muscles." The cardiovascular system is not a mirror muscle. Ultra training produces adaptation, not cosmetic pump. scl-directory.md: 🪞 definition. |
| 🗿 Sculpt | -4 | "Hypertrophy shaping. Angles, tension, volume." Sculpting is a tissue-specific hypertrophy goal. Ultra does not target individual muscle architecture. scl-directory.md: 🗿 definition. |
