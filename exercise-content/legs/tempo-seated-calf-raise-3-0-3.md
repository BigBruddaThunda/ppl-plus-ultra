# Tempo Seated Calf Raise (3-0-3)

## What It Is

Tempo Seated Calf Raise (3-0-3) is a leg isolation movement that primarily trains gastrocnemius, soleus. It sits in the Legs type of the SCL system and is classified as an isolation movement. 

## Setup

- Seat position: thigh pad just above the knee (leg extension) or ankle pad just above heel (leg curl).
- No grip death: hands rest lightly on handles.
- Full range available: do not limit ROM artificially.
- Slow enough to feel the target muscle — not just moving the weight.

## Execution

- Drive through the full range — full extension on leg extension, full curl on leg curl.
- Slow eccentric: 2–3 seconds down.
- No bouncing at the end range.
- Hold at the peak for 1 second to maximize muscular contraction.
- Keep the non-target segments still.

## Common Faults

- **Partial range — not extending fully:** Full extension is the stimulus for the quad. Half reps train the middle of the range only.
- **Bouncing at end range:** Control the return. The eccentric is the muscle-building component.

## What It Trains

**Primary:** gastrocnemius, soleus
**Secondary:** tibialis_posterior
**Joint actions:** ankle_plantarflexion
