# Dead Hang

## What It Is

Dead Hang is a vertical pull movement that primarily trains latissimus_dorsi. It sits in the Pull type of the SCL system and is classified as a compound movement. 

## Setup

- Grip slightly wider than shoulder width, hands pronated or neutral.
- Arms fully extended, shoulders elevated — start from a dead hang.
- Depress the scapulae before the first movement — lat engagement before arm pull.
- Look forward, not up — cervical spine neutral.

## Execution

- Initiate with scapular depression — shoulder blades pull down before arms pull.
- Chest moves toward the bar — not the bar to the chin.
- Elbows drive toward the hips in the bottom position.
- Full dead hang between reps (strict style).
- Controlled descent — not dropping.

## Common Faults

- **Chin-up instead of chest-up:** The chest moves toward the bar — not the chin. This changes the shoulder position entirely.
- **Kipping without intent:** Strict reps first. If kipping is in the plan, it must be controlled and intentional.
- **Partial range of motion:** Dead hang start is mandatory for lat stimulus. No half reps.

## What It Trains

**Primary:** latissimus_dorsi
**Secondary:** biceps_brachii, rear_deltoid, rhomboids, teres_major
**Joint actions:** shoulder_adduction, elbow_flexion, scapular_depression

## PPL± Context

**In Foundation (🐂):** Scapular depression before arm pull. The lat initiates — not the bicep. Foundation load means you can practice the activation pattern before load makes it irrelevant.

**In Strength (⛽):** Weighted pull-ups and heavy lat pulldowns demand full spinal extension at the top and controlled descent. The negative is strength work. Dropping the weight after the pull is waste.

**In Hypertrophy (🦋):** Full dead hang to chin-over-bar. The stretch at the bottom is the stimulus. Partial range pulldowns accumulate volume but not the full stretch reflex that drives lat hypertrophy.

**In Restoration (🖼):** Band-assisted pull-up or light cable pulldown. The lat is a respiratory muscle. Easy vertical pulls in restoration decompress the spine and restore shoulder girdle mobility.

**Color modifiers:**
- Technical (🟣): Dead-hang start. Full lockout extension before the next rep. The technical standard: the lat must engage before the elbow bends.
- Intense (🔴): Pull-up cluster sets or lat pulldown drop sets. Accumulate vertical pull volume with reduced rest.
- Mindful (⚪): 4-second descent to full dead hang. No kipping. No swinging. Exhale on the pull. The lat drives — not the bicep.
