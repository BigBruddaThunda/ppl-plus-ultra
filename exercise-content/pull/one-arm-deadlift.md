# One-Arm Deadlift

## What It Is

One-Arm Deadlift is a hip hinge movement that primarily trains core, transversus_abdominis, rectus_abdominis. It sits in the Pull type of the SCL system and is classified as a compound movement. 

## Setup

- Unilateral: complete all reps on one side before switching.
- Find neutral spine — not flat back, not excessive arch.
- 360-degree brace: belly out, back wide, pelvic floor engaged.
- Shoulders packed and away from the ears.
- Breath: in through the nose, brace, move. Exhale on the effort or hold.

## Execution

- Build pressure before moving — brace first, move second.
- Maintain neutral spine throughout — no spinal flexion/extension under load.
- Breathe: in through the nose, brace, move. Exhale on the effort.
- If position is lost, reset before continuing.
- Quality over time: a 20-second perfect plank beats a 60-second sagging one.

## Common Faults

- **Holding breath instead of bracing:** Brace first, breathe into the brace. They are separate actions. Holding breath is a Valsalva choice for max effort, not the default.
- **Hips hiking in plank or dead bug:** Find neutral pelvis first. The low back should have a slight natural curve, not flatten completely.
- **Arms/legs moving before core is set:** Setup order: position, brace, breathe, move. Never the reverse.

## What It Trains

**Primary:** core, transversus_abdominis, rectus_abdominis
**Secondary:** obliques, erector_spinae, hip_flexors
**Joint actions:** spinal_flexion, spinal_stabilization, intra_abdominal_pressure

## PPL± Context

**In Foundation (🐂):** Hinge mechanics take priority over load. Sub-65% means you have room to focus on the hip push-back, the hamstring load, and the neutral spine position. This is where the pattern is encoded.

**In Strength (⛽):** The hinge is a force-production pattern. At 75–85%, every rep is a neural event. Full setup before each pull. The bar doesn't move until the lats are locked and the floor is about to break.

**In Hypertrophy (🦋):** Drive hamstring tension through the full range. The concentric is deliberate — not explosive. Time under tension through the hip extension is what produces posterior chain adaptation here.

**In Restoration (🖼):** Hip-hinge patterns at restoration load (≤55%) become positional resets. RDL variations, light good mornings, or cable pull-throughs. If loading this pattern causes soreness the next day at restoration intensity, something else is wrong.

**Color modifiers:**
- Technical (🟣): Lower volume, longer rest, spotter or camera for form review. Every rep is a recorded event. The technical lens demands lockout position be perfect before adding load.
- Intense (🔴): Deadlift clusters, rest-pause sets, or heavy RDL dropsets. High mechanical demand combined with reduced rest — used sparingly. The hip-hinge earns intensity cautiously.
- Mindful (⚪): 4-second eccentric on every rep. The bar moves down with control, not gravity. Breath pattern: brace on the descent, exhale through the extension. The hamstring load is the cue.
