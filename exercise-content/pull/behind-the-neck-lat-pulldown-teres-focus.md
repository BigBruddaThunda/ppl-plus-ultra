# Behind-the-Neck Lat Pulldown (Teres Focus)

## What It Is

Behind-the-Neck Lat Pulldown (Teres Focus) is a vertical pull movement that primarily trains core, transversus_abdominis, rectus_abdominis. It sits in the Pull type of the SCL system and is classified as an isolation movement. 

## Setup

- Find neutral spine — not flat back, not excessive arch.
- 360-degree brace: belly out, back wide, pelvic floor engaged.
- Shoulders packed and away from the ears.
- Breath: in through the nose, brace, move. Exhale on the effort or hold.

## Execution

- Build pressure before moving — brace first, move second.
- Maintain neutral spine throughout — no spinal flexion/extension under load.
- Breathe: in through the nose, brace, move. Exhale on the effort.
- If position is lost, reset before continuing.
- Quality over time: a 20-second perfect plank beats a 60-second sagging one.

## Common Faults

- **Holding breath instead of bracing:** Brace first, breathe into the brace. They are separate actions. Holding breath is a Valsalva choice for max effort, not the default.
- **Hips hiking in plank or dead bug:** Find neutral pelvis first. The low back should have a slight natural curve, not flatten completely.
- **Arms/legs moving before core is set:** Setup order: position, brace, breathe, move. Never the reverse.

## What It Trains

**Primary:** core, transversus_abdominis, rectus_abdominis
**Secondary:** obliques, erector_spinae, hip_flexors
**Joint actions:** spinal_flexion, spinal_stabilization, intra_abdominal_pressure

## PPL± Context

**In Foundation (🐂):** Scapular depression before arm pull. The lat initiates — not the bicep. Foundation load means you can practice the activation pattern before load makes it irrelevant.

**In Strength (⛽):** Weighted pull-ups and heavy lat pulldowns demand full spinal extension at the top and controlled descent. The negative is strength work. Dropping the weight after the pull is waste.

**In Hypertrophy (🦋):** Full dead hang to chin-over-bar. The stretch at the bottom is the stimulus. Partial range pulldowns accumulate volume but not the full stretch reflex that drives lat hypertrophy.

**In Restoration (🖼):** Band-assisted pull-up or light cable pulldown. The lat is a respiratory muscle. Easy vertical pulls in restoration decompress the spine and restore shoulder girdle mobility.

**Color modifiers:**
- Technical (🟣): Dead-hang start. Full lockout extension before the next rep. The technical standard: the lat must engage before the elbow bends.
- Intense (🔴): Pull-up cluster sets or lat pulldown drop sets. Accumulate vertical pull volume with reduced rest.
- Mindful (⚪): 4-second descent to full dead hang. No kipping. No swinging. Exhale on the pull. The lat drives — not the bicep.
