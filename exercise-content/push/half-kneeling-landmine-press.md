# Half-Kneeling Landmine Press

## What It Is

Half-Kneeling Landmine Press is a vertical press movement that primarily trains core, transversus_abdominis, rectus_abdominis. It sits in the Push type of the SCL system and is classified as a compound movement. 

## Setup

- Unilateral: complete all reps on one side before switching.
- Find neutral spine — not flat back, not excessive arch.
- 360-degree brace: belly out, back wide, pelvic floor engaged.
- Shoulders packed and away from the ears.
- Breath: in through the nose, brace, move. Exhale on the effort or hold.

## Execution

- Build pressure before moving — brace first, move second.
- Maintain neutral spine throughout — no spinal flexion/extension under load.
- Breathe: in through the nose, brace, move. Exhale on the effort.
- If position is lost, reset before continuing.
- Quality over time: a 20-second perfect plank beats a 60-second sagging one.

## Common Faults

- **Holding breath instead of bracing:** Brace first, breathe into the brace. They are separate actions. Holding breath is a Valsalva choice for max effort, not the default.
- **Hips hiking in plank or dead bug:** Find neutral pelvis first. The low back should have a slight natural curve, not flatten completely.
- **Arms/legs moving before core is set:** Setup order: position, brace, breathe, move. Never the reverse.

## What It Trains

**Primary:** core, transversus_abdominis, rectus_abdominis
**Secondary:** obliques, erector_spinae, hip_flexors
**Joint actions:** spinal_flexion, spinal_stabilization, intra_abdominal_pressure

## Ppl± Context

**In Foundation (🐂):** Overhead position demands shoulder mobility that most people have to earn. Foundation load lets you find the path before you fight the load. Lockout position is the cue — stack the joint.

**In Strength (⛽):** The strict press is an honesty test. At 75–85%, the body wants to use the legs. Keep them out. The movement starts at the chest and finishes with ears through the arms. Each rep is its own event.

**In Hypertrophy (🦋):** Partial range presses and lateral raise variations accumulate shoulder volume effectively. The medial delt responds to mechanical tension, not just metabolic stress. 8–12 reps with genuine shoulder isolation.

**In Restoration (🖼):** Face pulls, band YTWLs, or light lateral raises. The shoulder girdle at restoration intensity is being mobilized and maintained, not developed. Any pain here is signal — not to be pushed through.

**Color modifiers:**
- Technical (🟣): Strict press with submaximal load. No leg drive. The technical standard here is ears through the arms at lockout. Anything less is a partial rep.
- Intense (🔴): Press superset with lateral raise or face pull. Shoulder volume accumulated across planes with reduced rest.
- Mindful (⚪): 4-second eccentric from lockout to rack position. Exhale on the press. The shoulder joint is stacked — the wrist is over the elbow is over the shoulder.
