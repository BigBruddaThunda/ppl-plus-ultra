# Kettlebell Goblet Squat

## What It Is

Kettlebell Goblet Squat is a squat movement that primarily trains core, transversus_abdominis, rectus_abdominis. It sits in the Push type of the SCL system and is classified as a compound movement. 

## Setup

- Find neutral spine — not flat back, not excessive arch.
- 360-degree brace: belly out, back wide, pelvic floor engaged.
- Shoulders packed and away from the ears.
- Breath: in through the nose, brace, move. Exhale on the effort or hold.

## Execution

- Build pressure before moving — brace first, move second.
- Maintain neutral spine throughout — no spinal flexion/extension under load.
- Breathe: in through the nose, brace, move. Exhale on the effort.
- If position is lost, reset before continuing.
- Quality over time: a 20-second perfect plank beats a 60-second sagging one.

## Common Faults

- **Holding breath instead of bracing:** Brace first, breathe into the brace. They are separate actions. Holding breath is a Valsalva choice for max effort, not the default.
- **Hips hiking in plank or dead bug:** Find neutral pelvis first. The low back should have a slight natural curve, not flatten completely.
- **Arms/legs moving before core is set:** Setup order: position, brace, breathe, move. Never the reverse.

## What It Trains

**Primary:** core, transversus_abdominis, rectus_abdominis
**Secondary:** obliques, erector_spinae, hip_flexors
**Joint actions:** spinal_flexion, spinal_stabilization, intra_abdominal_pressure

## Ppl± Context

**In Foundation (🐂):** Foot width, bracing, and descent rate are the lesson. Load is irrelevant. The goal is groove — the same position rep after rep. Sub-65% gives you 8–15 reps to get it right.

**In Strength (⛽):** High CNS demand. The squat earns its intensity. 75–85% with a 3–4 minute rest is not optional — it is the mechanism. Cut the rest and you are no longer training strength.

**In Hypertrophy (🦋):** Quad emphasis through full depth and controlled descent. Pause at the bottom to eliminate the stretch reflex and force the quads to do the work. Pump is information — not the goal, but a signal.

**In Restoration (🖼):** Goblet squat or bodyweight box squat. Mobility work masquerading as strength. The joint is being asked to move, not loaded. Any squat in restoration should leave the tissues better than you found them.

**Color modifiers:**
- Technical (🟣): Tempo squats, pause squats, or single-leg progressions. The technical lens strips load and demands position. Every deviation is information.
- Intense (🔴): Drop sets, cluster sets, or squat supersets (squat → leg press → hack squat). The quad accumulates work across multiple angles with reduced rest.
- Mindful (⚪): 4-second descent, 2-second pause at the bottom, controlled rise. Breath pattern: brace before descent, exhale past parallel. The hip crease below parallel is the cue.
