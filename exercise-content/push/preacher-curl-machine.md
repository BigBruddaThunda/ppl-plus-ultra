# Preacher Curl Machine

## What It Is

Preacher Curl Machine is a isolation curl movement that primarily trains core, transversus_abdominis, rectus_abdominis. It sits in the Push type of the SCL system and is classified as an isolation movement. 

## Setup

- Find neutral spine — not flat back, not excessive arch.
- 360-degree brace: belly out, back wide, pelvic floor engaged.
- Shoulders packed and away from the ears.
- Breath: in through the nose, brace, move. Exhale on the effort or hold.

## Execution

- Build pressure before moving — brace first, move second.
- Maintain neutral spine throughout — no spinal flexion/extension under load.
- Breathe: in through the nose, brace, move. Exhale on the effort.
- If position is lost, reset before continuing.
- Quality over time: a 20-second perfect plank beats a 60-second sagging one.

## Common Faults

- **Holding breath instead of bracing:** Brace first, breathe into the brace. They are separate actions. Holding breath is a Valsalva choice for max effort, not the default.
- **Hips hiking in plank or dead bug:** Find neutral pelvis first. The low back should have a slight natural curve, not flatten completely.
- **Arms/legs moving before core is set:** Setup order: position, brace, breathe, move. Never the reverse.

## What It Trains

**Primary:** core, transversus_abdominis, rectus_abdominis
**Secondary:** obliques, erector_spinae, hip_flexors
**Joint actions:** spinal_flexion, spinal_stabilization, intra_abdominal_pressure

## Ppl± Context

**In Foundation (🐂):** Supination through the full range of motion — this is the lesson at foundation load. Elbow position does not change. The weight is a prop for practicing the contraction.

**In Strength (⛽):** Heavy curls are not conventional strength work, but preacher curls and incline curls at challenging loads (relatively) develop the long head of the bicep through stretch-mediated hypertrophy.

**In Hypertrophy (🦋):** The bicep responds to peak contraction and full stretch. Incline dumbbell curls, preacher curls, and cross-body variations each bias a different part of the range. Accumulated sets with controlled tempo.

**In Restoration (🖼):** Light band curls or light cable curls at full extension. The elbow flexors are maintained, not loaded. Any restoration curl is about joint health and circulation, not development.

**Color modifiers:**
- Technical (🟣): Strict supination. Elbow stays pinned. The weight is secondary to the quality of the curl path.
- Intense (🔴): Bicep superset: incline curl into preacher curl into standing curl. Three angles, minimal rest, full pump.
- Mindful (⚪): 4-second eccentric from peak contraction to full extension. Pause at the bottom before the next rep. Exhale on the curl.
