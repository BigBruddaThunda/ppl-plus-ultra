# Dumbbell Floor Press

## What It Is

Dumbbell Floor Press is a horizontal press movement that primarily trains pectoralis_major. It sits in the Push type of the SCL system and is classified as a compound movement. 

## Setup

- Shoulders retracted and depressed against the pad — create tension before you touch the bar.
- 5-point contact: feet on floor, glutes on pad, upper back on pad, head on pad.
- Grip just outside shoulder width (flat press) or shoulder width (close grip).
- Air in before unrack. Hold through the descent.

## Execution

- Bar path slightly diagonal: touch lower chest, press to over the face.
- Elbows at 45–75° to the torso — not flared perpendicular.
- Touch the chest: controlled, not bounced.
- Press through the heel of the hand.
- Lockout: elbow slightly soft, not hyperextended.

## Common Faults

- **Elbows flaring perpendicular to the torso:** 45–75° is the healthy angle. Flared elbows load the anterior shoulder capsule.
- **Bar bouncing off the chest:** Touch and go is a technique choice — not a compensation. Control the descent.
- **Wrists breaking back under load:** Bar in the heel of the hand, not the palm. Thumbs around the bar.
- **Butt coming off the pad:** Foot position and leg drive may be excessive. Re-establish 5-point contact.

## What It Trains

**Primary:** pectoralis_major
**Secondary:** anterior_deltoid, triceps_brachii
**Joint actions:** shoulder_horizontal_adduction, elbow_extension

## PPL± Context

**In Foundation (🐂):** Scapular position before bar position. The shoulder blade must be set before the chest is loaded. Foundation load means you have time to find the position without chasing a number.

**In Strength (⛽):** Leg drive is not optional at 75–85%. The press is a full-body movement at strength intensity. The bar path is straight. The wrists are stacked. The arch is earned, not cheated.

**In Hypertrophy (🦋):** Full pec stretch at the bottom. Controlled descent. No bounce. The tricep finishes, but the pec drives. Accumulated sets of 8–12 with genuine tension throughout — not just moving through range.

**In Restoration (🖼):** Light cable flyes, band chest presses, or low-incline machine press. The chest is active, not loaded. Restoration context means no heavy press variation — the tissue needs circulation, not stimulus.

**Color modifiers:**
- Technical (🟣): Paused press, tempo press, or board press. The technical lens exposes bar path deviation. Quality is the only metric.
- Intense (🔴): Bench press supersets or tri-sets (flat → incline → cable fly). High volume, reduced rest, accumulated chest work across three planes.
- Mindful (⚪): 4-second eccentric to the chest. The bar does not bounce. Exhale on the press. Feel the pec stretch at the bottom — that stretch is the point.
