# Dragon Flag

## What It Is

Dragon Flag is a core stability movement that primarily trains core, transversus_abdominis, rectus_abdominis. It sits in the Plus type of the SCL system and is classified as an isolation movement. 

## Setup

- Find neutral spine — not flat back, not excessive arch.
- 360-degree brace: belly out, back wide, pelvic floor engaged.
- Shoulders packed and away from the ears.
- Breath: in through the nose, brace, move. Exhale on the effort or hold.

## Execution

- Build pressure before moving — brace first, move second.
- Maintain neutral spine throughout — no spinal flexion/extension under load.
- Breathe: in through the nose, brace, move. Exhale on the effort.
- If position is lost, reset before continuing.
- Quality over time: a 20-second perfect plank beats a 60-second sagging one.

## Common Faults

- **Holding breath instead of bracing:** Brace first, breathe into the brace. They are separate actions. Holding breath is a Valsalva choice for max effort, not the default.
- **Hips hiking in plank or dead bug:** Find neutral pelvis first. The low back should have a slight natural curve, not flatten completely.
- **Arms/legs moving before core is set:** Setup order: position, brace, breathe, move. Never the reverse.

## PPL± Context

**In Foundation (🐂):** Bracing mechanics are the lesson. Foundation load in core-stability work is often bodyweight. The goal is teaching the nervous system to generate intra-abdominal pressure on demand.

**In Strength (⛽):** Weighted core stability — heavy rollouts, loaded planks, or cable anti-rotation with added load — extends the core stability pattern into strength territory. Limited exercises qualify.

**In Hypertrophy (🦋):** Core exercises at 8–15 rep ranges with intentional time under tension produce endurance-hypertrophy. The deep core structures respond to duration, not load.

**In Restoration (🖼):** Diaphragmatic breathing, supine dead bug, and child's pose spinal decompression. Restoration core work is nervous system regulation, not muscular development.

**Color modifiers:**
- Technical (🟣): Lower volume, longer rest, quality over speed. The technical lens means every rep is examined and every deviation is corrected.
- Intense (🔴): Higher volume, reduced rest, possible supersets. The intensity lens means accumulating work — not just completing it.
- Mindful (⚪): 4-second eccentric phase. Deliberate breathing. Extended rest. The mindful lens turns outward effort inward.
