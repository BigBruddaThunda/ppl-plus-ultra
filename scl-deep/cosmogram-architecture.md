## What a Cosmogram Is

A cosmogram is the knowledge substrate of a deck. It maps the full scope of human knowledge —
training, philosophy, architecture, ecology, history, music, language, community, time, craft,
food, law, astronomy, economics, medicine, pedagogy, technology, and any domain where ideas live —
into a single deck's gravitational field using the 61 SCL emojis as organizational branches.

Each of the 42 decks (7 Orders × 6 Axes) receives one cosmogram. Each cosmogram contains 61
branches — one per SCL emoji. The branches are shelves. Knowledge deposits are what sits on them.

The cosmogram is DNA, not the organism. Users never read cosmograms directly. The rooms
cosmograms inform are what users experience — through card content, Operis editorials, Wilson
Notes, community seeding, and almanac entries.

## Why 61 Branches

The 61 SCL emojis are already a complete organizational vocabulary for describing experience.
Each emoji carries meaning along multiple registers simultaneously:

- **As training parameter** — What this emoji controls in a workout.
- **As developmental phase** — Where something is in its lifecycle.
- **As dimensional lens** — What question this emoji asks of reality.
- **As temperament** — What cognitive posture this emoji invites.
- **As process container** — What phase of work this emoji names.
- **As action verb** — What operation this emoji performs.
- **As seasonal marker** — When in the year this emoji's energy peaks.
- **As architectural register** — What classical tradition this emoji evokes.

A cosmogram maps knowledge into ALL of these registers simultaneously, because the SCL is
polysemic by design — the same emoji holds different valid meanings depending on context, and
the cosmogram IS the document where all those meanings converge.

## The Polysemy Principle

Each emoji means what it means **in the context of the deck it belongs to**.

🌋 Gutter at a 🖼 Restoration deck is not about all-out physical effort. It is about the edge
of complete surrender — the moment recovery crosses into dissolution, the thin line between
letting go and giving up. The knowledge deposits on this branch would include contemplative
traditions around ego death, agricultural practices of letting fields go fallow past the point
of comfort, architectural demolition as creative act.

🧈 Bread & Butter at a ⚖ Balance deck is not the heaviest lift. It is the corrective compound
that addresses the specific gap. The knowledge deposits would include asymmetry in classical
sculpture, counterpoint in music theory, the concept of "balancing the books" in accounting,
proprioceptive training in rehabilitation science.

The deck's Order × Axis intersection is the gravitational center. Every emoji bends toward that
center. The reinterpretation IS the cosmogram.

## The 61 Emojis — Deep Register

Each emoji below carries its training meaning (from `scl-directory.md`) plus its broader
registers. The broader registers are NOT separate from the training meanings — they are the same
meaning operating at different scales.

These are six fundamental questions you can ask of ANY subject. They derive from Vitruvius's

## File Format

**Path:** `deck-cosmograms/deck-[NUMBER]-cosmogram-v2.md`

Every v2 cosmogram follows the same structure so scripts can parse them.

### Header (YAML frontmatter)

```yaml

```

### Branch Format (consistent across all 42 files)

```markdown
## [EMOJI] [Name] — [What this branch means at THIS deck's address]

**Affinity:** [weight-derived label]

Core resonance: [1–2 sentences: why this emoji matters for this deck]

Knowledge deposits:
- [Specific name, tradition, concept, reference, date, person, place]
- [Another specific deposit]

Cross-cosmogram threads:
- → Deck [XX] ([reason])

Open questions:
- [What needs more research]
```

The `## [EMOJI]` header is the parsing contract. Every branch begins with `## ` followed by the
emoji. Scripts key on this pattern.

## Density Expectations

Not all branches are equally rich at every deck. This is correct and honest. Expected density
patterns:

- The deck's **own Order emoji**: always rich. This is home territory.
- The deck's **own Axis emoji**: always rich. This is the primary lens.
- The deck's **paired Operators**: naturally rich. These are the action verbs.
- Adjacent Orders (±1): moderately rich. Neighboring phases connect.
- Opposing Axes: potentially rich through contrast.
- Blocks prominent in this Order's workout structure: rich.
- Blocks that rarely appear in this Order: thin — and that is informative.

A branch with 2 deposits is honest. A branch padded to 10 deposits with filler is
architecturally unsound. **Thin branches are data** — they tell the system where knowledge
naturally concentrates and where it does not.

## Cross-Cosmogram Threading

Every branch can reference other decks' cosmograms. The threading format:

```
→ Deck [XX] ([emoji] — [reason])
```

Threads create the nervous system connecting all 42 cosmograms. A script can harvest all threads
and build a 42×42 adjacency matrix showing which decks are most connected through shared
knowledge.

**Natural thread patterns:**

- **Same-Order decks** share Order-branch content (all 6 decks in the ⛽ row share ⛽ branch
  knowledge, viewed through different Axis lenses)
- **Same-Axis decks** share Axis-branch content (all 7 decks in the 🏛 column share 🏛 branch
  knowledge, viewed at different developmental phases)
- **Operator-paired decks** connect through their shared Latin verb
- **Thematic connections** that emerge during research — the most valuable threads

**Deck adjacency formula:**

Given deck number D (1–42):
- Same-Order decks: `{D - ((D-1) % 6) + k | k in 0..5} \ {D}`  →  the 5 other decks in D's row
- Same-Axis decks: `{((D-1) % 6) + 1 + k*6 | k in 0..6} \ {D}`  →  the 6 other decks in D's column
- Deck derivation: `deck = (order - 1) * 6 + axis`

## Relationship to Existing Stubs

The 42 files in `deck-cosmograms/` (without `-v2` suffix) use a domain-based section format:
Movement & Physical Culture, Architecture & Design, Agriculture & Ecology, etc. That format was
an earlier attempt at what the 61-branch system does more precisely — the emojis ARE the
domains, and they are consistent across all 42 files.

**Migration approach:**

- The scaffold script (`scripts/scaffold-cosmograms-v2.py`) generates new `-v2` files alongside
  existing stubs, migrating frontmatter from the old stubs.
- Both files coexist until research population begins on the v2 file.
- Once a v2 cosmogram is populated (status: POPULATED or REVIEWED), the old stub is archived.
- The v2 file is the authoritative format going forward.

## Anti-Filler Principles

These apply at population time (during research sessions).

Every deposit is **specific**. Names. Dates. Places. Titles. No "many traditions share this
view." Name the traditions.

No motivational language. No wellness vocabulary. No inspirational framing. The cosmogram is a
reference document, not a sermon.

A thin branch is honest data. Do not pad. A branch with 1 deposit and the note "thin at this
address — concentrates at Deck XX" is more valuable than a branch padded with vague
generalizations.

**Cathedral thinking.** Would this deposit still matter in 50 years? If it is trending-topic
knowledge, it probably does not belong. If it has been true for 500 years, it probably does.

No single branch should dominate. The 61-branch structure prevents any one research thread from
becoming the whole cosmogram. If you have written 500 words on one branch and 10 on the other
60, the structure is wrong.

Cross-cosmogram threads are not optional. Every populated branch should identify at least one
connection to another deck. The threads ARE the nervous system.
