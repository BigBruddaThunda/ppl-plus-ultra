## The Voice, Restated

The voice is the building's voice. Measured, technical, human, neutral. It speaks to a competent adult who does not need to be managed. It is readable by a literate teenager and a retired architect in the same edition without either cringing.

The voice does not perform enthusiasm. It does not coach through encouragement. It reports, specifies, observes, and names. It earns warmth through attention, not adjectives.

## The Factual Register

Every claim about exercise, anatomy, or training effect must be traceable to exercise science. The standard is a good textbook written warmly â€” not a peer-reviewed abstract, but nothing a peer reviewer would contradict.

**Examples**:

Acceptable: "The Romanian deadlift loads the posterior chain through hip extension under an eccentric stretch of the hamstrings."

Banned: "The Romanian deadlift transforms your backside."

Acceptable: "Five sets of five at 80% builds force production through high-threshold motor unit recruitment."

Banned: "Five sets of five optimizes neuromuscular recruitment."

Acceptable: "The latissimus dorsi is the primary mover in vertical pulling. It originates at the thoracolumbar fascia and inserts at the intertubercular groove of the humerus."

Banned: "Pull training builds that V-taper."

The anatomical claim should name what is working and how, not what it looks like or what it does for appearance. Appearance references belong in ðŸŒ¹ Aesthetic content and ðŸªž Vanity blocks â€” and even there, the language is honest and specific, not aspirational.

## Tonal Prohibitions

These are categorical prohibitions, not word-level bans. Any content that falls into one of these categories fails regardless of the specific words used.

**Motivational filler**: Content whose purpose is to make the reader feel good rather than to convey useful information. The test: if the sentence were deleted, would the reader lose any information? If no, delete it.

**Clinical jargon**: Anatomical terms deployed to sound authoritative rather than to convey precision. If a phrase like "activate the posterior kinetic chain" could be replaced with "extend at the hip" without loss of meaning, replace it.

**Spiritual-wellness register**: Any language that implies training is a path to self-discovery, inner peace, or transformation of the self. Training adapts tissue. It does not transform identity.

**Patronizing framing**: Content that addresses the reader as if they need to be managed, encouraged, or protected from the difficulty of the work. The reader is a competent adult. Treat them accordingly.

**AI-generated list cadence**: Bullet-point lists that substitute enumeration for thought. A list is appropriate when the content is genuinely enumerable. It is not appropriate as a substitute for prose that would require thinking.

## The Newspaper Test

Before publishing any sentence, ask: would a copy editor at a serious newspaper let this through?

The copy editor's standard:
- Is every claim supported by a fact?
- Does every sentence say one thing?
- Is there any word that could be cut without losing meaning?
- Would the sentence embarrass the publication if read aloud?

If any answer points to a problem, revise.
