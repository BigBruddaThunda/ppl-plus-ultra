# What is Ppl±?

Ppl± (pronounced "people plus") is a training system with 1,680 unique workouts, each identified by a 4-emoji address called a zip code.

Every zip code specifies exactly what you're doing: how heavy, what style, which muscles, and what equipment. No guessing. No browsing. You get an address, you open the room, you train.

## How It Works

The system has four dials:

**Order** — How heavy and how hard. Seven levels, from gentle coaching sessions to max-effort testing days. The Order sets the ceiling. Nothing exceeds it.

**Axis** — What kind of exercises. Classic barbell lifts? Unilateral athletic movements? Isolation and mind-muscle work? Hardest possible variations? Time-based protocols? Partner exercises? Six different characters.

**Type** — Which muscles. Push (chest, shoulders, triceps), Pull (back, biceps), Legs, Plus (power and core), or Ultra (conditioning and cardio). Five movement domains.

**Color** — What equipment and what format. Bodyweight only? Full gym with prescribed sets and reps? Maximum intensity with supersets? Circuit training? Eight different formats, from a park bench to a fully equipped gym.

Combine the four dials: 7 × 6 × 5 × 8 = 1,680 unique workouts.

## What a Workout Looks Like

Each workout is a sequence of blocks — rooms inside the session. A warm-up, a main working section, supplemental work, and a junction that bridges you to your next session. Every block has exercises selected specifically for the zip code's constraints. Every set has its load, reps, and coaching cues.

The system writes the workout for you. You show up and train.

## Training Programs

Ppl± organizes its 1,680 workouts into 35 training programs called abaci. Each abacus is a complete training program built from a specific combination of the four dials.

Want to train like a powerlifter? There's an abacus for that — heavy loads, barbell classics, full recovery. Want bodyweight-only training you can do anywhere? There's an abacus for that. Want a mix of strength and conditioning? Combine two abaci and the system handles the rotation.

Each abacus contains 48 workouts. The rotation engine cycles through them based on the day of the week, a 5-day rolling calendar, and the month. You never do the same workout twice in a 35-day cycle.

## What Ppl± Is Not

It's not a workout video platform. There are no instructors performing exercises on camera.

It's not a social fitness app. There are community features, but the system is built around the training, not the feed.

It's not a generic template. Every one of the 1,680 zip codes produces a different workout with different exercises, different loading, different structure. The differences are real and derived from the address.

## The Name

Ppl± reads as "People Plus." The ± means every workout can go both directions — harder or easier, heavier or lighter, more or less. The full name is ppl-plus-ultra: people, plus, beyond.
